How to Register & Timeline

Everything you need to know to participate in Hult Prize NIT Durgapur

How to Register?

1

Form Your Team

Gather a team of 3-4 passionate members who share your vision for social entrepreneurship. Diversity in skills and perspectives strengthens your team.

2

Team Leader Signs Up

The team leader creates an account on our platform and registers the team with all member details. You'll receive a verification email to confirm your registration.

3

Team Members Accept Invitation

All team members will receive an invitation email. They must accept the invitation to be officially added to the team roster.

4

Or Join an Existing Team

Don't have a complete team? No problem! Register individually and send requests to join other teams. You can send one request every 24 hours.

Each team must have a minimum of 3 members and maximum of 4 members. All team members must be current students at NIT Durgapur.

Competition Timeline

November 1-3, 2024

Pre-Hult Event

Join us for an introductory event to learn about the Hult Prize 2025, meet past winners, and understand what it takes to succeed in the competition.

December 6, 2024

On Campus Orientation

Mandatory orientation session for all registered teams. Learn about the competition format, judging criteria, and get your questions answered.

December 10-11, 2024

Speaker Sessions

Gain insights from industry experts and successful entrepreneurs who will share their experiences and knowledge about building impactful startups.

December 14-16, 2024

Mentor Sessions

Get personalized guidance from experienced mentors to refine your business idea, pitch, and presentation strategy.

December 19, 2024 - Deadline

Registrations Close

Final date to register your team for the competition. Make sure all team details are submitted and verified before the deadline.

December 20-22, 2024

On Campus Round

The main event! Pitch your innovative idea to a panel of distinguished judges. Winners will advance to represent NIT Durgapur at Regional Finals.

Winning teams from the on-campus round will advance to compete at the Regional Finals, held in one of six countries worldwide. Regional winners then proceed to the Global Finals for a chance to win the $1 million prize!
